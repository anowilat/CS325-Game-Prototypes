Crash

  • High Concept:
    Players race against two computer-controlled cars, however they must abide by the rules of the road;
    making sure to observe the stoplight or risk crashing into another car.

  • Theme:
    • Car accident

  • Mandated Variety:
    • Input: Clickable Buttons
    • Randomness: Some Randomness
    • Play styles: --
    • Genres: Racer

  • Player Experience Goals:
    • Experience a 2D fast-paced racer; where players control (albiet limitedly) their own vehicles.
    • Suspense and risk; players must make quick decisions on how to drive and whether they risk themselves.
    • Scoreboarding and oppurtunity to score better on subsequent trials.

  • Gameplay:
    Players race against two computer-controlled cars and must survive a 10 lap race by following the rules or
    risking themselves. As players lap through, a stoplight will call a specific signal color: green, yellow, red
    at each lap. The breakdown of the signals is as follows:
    • Green: Players may pass without fear of crashing.
    • Red: Players must stop or they will crash.
    • Yellow: Players have the option to pass onto the next block or stop, but there is a 50/50 chance of crashing.

  • Prototype Goal:
    • To develop a digital game based off mechanics of a physical prototype.
    • To combine skills learned from all prototypes into a single product.

  • Features:
    This prototype uses various assets for visual appeal, but ultimately was more code-intensive than previous protypes.
    Most of the development time was spent on features such as driving, opponent AI, crashing rate, lap wrapping...etc.
    There are a few bugs and kinks to work put with regards to opponent AI and their driving/stopping mechanics. There
    is also room for more player options such as car color, number of laps, and types of races, that will be tested in
    future iterations.
    NOTE: the restart button has been added but not yet implemented.

  • Setting/Premise:
    The setting is potentially suburban for this prototype, but future iterations might move it to urban areas.
    The initial premise for the game was that of a street, rather than professional, type race.

  • Story:
    Prototype has no story other than the race itself.

  • Target Audience:
    This game is suitable for all ages, although some might feel it needs parental guidance regarding the car crashes.
    Futhermore, this game might be interesting for gamers who enjoy racer games.

  • Play Time:
    A single race often takes upwards of 5 minutes, but can be shorter or longer than that. Future iterations will
    add a "number of laps" option for players to either increase or decrease playtime.

  • Strategies:
    Opponent cars start immediately and there is no lag time with their driving, so players must be quick about starting
    their vehicle when stopped, since there is slight a acceleration lag. Players who play the game enough notice
    incoming cars faster and can then hit the brake faster.

  • Prototype Assets:
    The full list of borrowed and modified assets include:
    • Car Model
    • Grass Tile
    • Road Tile
    • Explosion Animation
    • Accelerate/Brake Buttons
    • All audio assets

  • Prototype Closest Other Game:

    • Prototype takes the main mechanic (crash scenarios and light signal) from the physical prototype. Rather than
    make the game turn-based, the mechanics are now in real-time. The physical game was also playable by 2 - 3 people,
    however the digital version is a single player game with AI cars replacing other players.
