Wheel of Misfortune

  • High Concept:
    A pesky chameleon has somehow landed on the wheel of a guessing game. Players help the
    little guy out by choosing the proper color he should be when the wheel stops.

  • Theme:
    • Guessing Game Gone Wrong
    • Invisibility

  • Mandated Variety:
    • Input: Clickable Buttons
    • Randomness: A Lot of Randomness
    • Play styles: Joker
    • Genres: Arcade

  • Player Experience Goals:
    • Entertainment and humor; using a variety of colors to envoke fun and amusement.
    • Likeable maiin character; hopefully players develop a sense of attachment to the
      star chameleon and root for his succeess.

  • Gameplay:
    The wheel will contineously spin and repeatedly stop at a certain color, the player then
    has a limited amount of time to change the chameleons color by clicking on one of the
    on-screen buttons.

  • Prototype Goal:
    This prototype took a step back from exploring new Phaser features and instead focused on
    refining or further developing skills aquired. Features present in this prototype include:
    • More practice with animation and using specific frames for specific cases.
    • On-screen buttons and small interface/world design.
    • Working with Phaser Timer and Event functions and understanding when they are useful.

  • Features:
    The game is mostly a point-and-click interactive puzzle/arcade game. Most of the games elements
    are lighthearted and more effort was spent on desinging the premise in a unique and hopefuilly
    entertaining way.

  • Setting/Premise:
    The game is set on a game show stage when some technical difficulty has occured: a chameleon
    has somehow managed to climb onto the games spinning wheel.

  • Story:
    The story begins with a chameleon having managed to climb its way onto a game shows wheel, there
    it continues to change color depending on its background to camouflage itself from the audience.
    The story concludes with the chameleon being discovered.

  • Target Audience:
    This game is suitable for all ages and all demographics. Those with color blindness might have
    a difficult time telling the colors apart.

  • Play Time:
    This game is an arcade game and may take between seconds to minutes in gameplay.

  • Strategies:
    Players have 2 seconds to click on the matching wheel color on the left interface, so a good
    strategy would be to keep their mouse within that range.
