Reef Rescue

  • High Concept:
    The Great Barrier Reef is environmentally threatend by pollution, climate change, and overfishing;
    coral bleaching has hit an all time high, slowly starving and killing the reefs coral.
    Players take on the role of a underwater preservationist that routinely heals and maintains
    bleached coral under time constraints.

  • Theme:
    • Preservationist

  • Mandated Variety:
    • Input: Mouse
    • Randomness: Some Randomness
    • Play styles: Storyteller/Achiever
    • Genres: Arcade

  • Player Experience Goals:
    • Relation to real-life events; using the Great Barrier Reef as a premise/setting for the game.
    • World interactivity; a tilemap to limit player movement and coral as goals for the player.
    • Instilling a sense of responsibility; coral that permenantly die might increase tension.

  • Gameplay:
    Players point and hold the mouse button to direct the diver underwater.
    The coral the player must heal are either slightly moving/shimmering, or are bleached in color.
    To heal a coral, players must simply swim over the coral. If a coral turns black, it's dead.
    Coral color indicators are as followed:
    • Full color/Shimmering: alive and at full health. Health = 3.
    • Bleached color: alive but losing health. Health = 2 or Health = 1.
    • Black color: dead, cannot be healed. Health = 0.
    Players should keep in mind that the coral degrade randomly every 7 seconds.

  • Prototype Goal:
    The goal for this prototype was to take the plunge and experiment with TileMaps. The background,
    setting, and flora were designed using Tiled, with additional sprites such as the coral and the
    diver added as new entities with Phaser.

  • Features:
    Most of the game development was spent gaining an understanding of TileMaps and how to incorporate
    them into Phaser code. Gameplay is mostly simple, with the only difficulty being maintaining all
    the coral in the area. Inspiration for the gameplay was initially drawn from Snake.

  • Setting/Premise:
    The game takes place in the Great Barrier Reef off the coast of Australia, which has been suffering
    from numerous environmental threats within the last few years.

  • Story:
    As the coral of the Great Barrier Reef degrades, a wildlife preservationist and diver is charged
    with regular maintenance of the reef and its corals.

  • Target Audience:
    This game is suitable for all ages and all demographics.

  • Play Time:
    The play time for this game takes on average a few minutes, as the coral do not die all at once.

  • Strategies:
    There are six coral in the area, all of which degrade at random every 7 seconds. A random number
    generator uses the index of individual coral from the group coral, and decrease their health by 1.
    If a corals health = 2, the player still has time to heal the coral before the coral reaches
    health = 1, and subsequently dead at health = 0.
    A feature to be potentially added might be a small bar interface that tells the player what coral
    is a what health; however this might affect player gameplay and make it easier.
